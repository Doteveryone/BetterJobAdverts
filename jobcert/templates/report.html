Better Job Adverts

Poor quality job adverts fail job hunters and employers. This tool scans a job adverts. It tells employers how to improve and job hunters what to look out for.

We can use technology and open standards to make the job market better in the UK.

What are the problems?

  • Citizens Advice research has shown that job adverts in the UK are very poor quality. Information like salaries, geographical location, skills needed are unclear and inconsistent.
  • There is some evidence that 'gender coded language' in job adverts may influence if applicants feel they 'belonged' in that occupation.
  • Half of the UK working population have a reading age of 11-years-old or younger. Badly written or hard to understand job adverts make it harder for people to apply for them.
  • Unclear copyright status of job adverts and lack of structured data stifles reuse and innovation.

What does Better Job Adverts check for?

The schema.org jobPosting standard
The jobPosting standard can help solve some of the issues about poor quality job adverts by helping publishers to include structured information about things like pay and location. It also make it easier for search engines to find jobs.
Gender coded language
The job description is checked against a list of 'gender coded' words from Gender Decoder for Job Ads.
Readability
The job description is checked against the Flesch–Kincaid readability test.
Open licences
The advert is checked for evidence that it has been licensed under a Creative Commons licence that makes it clear how and where the advert may be republished.
